Namami Gange

Namami Gange is an integrated conservation mission launched by the Government of India in 2014, aimed at cleaning and rejuvenating the Ganga River. The initiative focuses on reducing pollution, enhancing waste management, and promoting community engagement to restore the river's ecological health. Objectives of Namami Gange Conservation and Rejuvenation: The primary goal is to conserve and rejuvenate the Ganga River, addressing the severe pollution it faces. Pollution Reduction: A significant focus is on reducing the pollution load, particularly from untreated wastewater and industrial effluents. Community Involvement: Engaging local communities and stakeholders is crucial for the success of the program, fostering a sense of ownership and responsibility. Implementation Strategy Phased Approach: The program is structured into entry-level, medium-term, and long-term activities, allowing for immediate impacts as well as sustainable long-term solutions. Collaboration: The initiative involves collaboration between various levels of government, including state governments, urban local bodies, and panchayati raj institutions. Key Features Financial Support: The program has a substantial budget of Rs. 20,000 crore, aimed at supporting state efforts in pollution control. Urban Planning: It promotes river-centric urban planning to enhance public access to the river and improve the overall environment around riverfronts and ghats. Integrated Approach: Namami Gange integrates previous initiatives and enhances them with better coordination and comprehensive interventions to maximize effectiveness.
